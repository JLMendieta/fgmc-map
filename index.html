Data Sources

UNICEF (2024) – Global Update

UNICEF 2024

UNICEF. 2024. Female Genital Mutilation: A Global Concern – 2024 Update. New York: UNICEF.

Nationally representative DHS/MICS household survey; prevalence among women 15–49 (and girls where available).

UNICEF / UNFPA – Country Profiles and Snapshots

UNICEF Country Profile  /  UNFPA Snapshot

UNICEF. Various years. Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting Country Profiles (DHS/MICS-based country briefs). New York: UNICEF.
UNFPA. 2023–2024. FGM Country Snapshot: [Country name]. New York: UNFPA.

Latest available DHS/MICS or equivalent national survey; directly reported prevalence.

Farouki et al. (2022) – Meta-analysis

Farouki et al. 2022

Farouki, L. et al. 2022. “The Global Prevalence of Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis.” PLOS Medicine 19(8): e1004061.

Pooled estimate from systematic review and meta-analysis (GLMM/random-effects) based on multiple surveys.

End FGM European Network, Equality Now & US End FGM/C Network (2025)

Time is Now 2025

End FGM European Network, Equality Now & US End FGM/C Network. 2025. The Time is Now: End Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting (FGM/C): An Urgent Need for Global Response. Brussels / London / Washington DC.

Where only presence is known: documented presence from case reports, legal/NGO, or small studies; no nationally representative prevalence. Where numeric prevalence is quoted (e.g. Malaysia, Indonesia): estimate from non-national or mixed study sources as compiled in this advocacy report.

Equality Now & End FGM European Network (2020)

Call for Global Response 2020

Equality Now & End FGM European Network. 2020. Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting: A Call for a Global Response. London / Brussels.

Compilation from existing surveys and secondary sources; used to identify affected countries rather than as a primary numeric source.
